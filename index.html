Stop selling the on-switch. Sell the off-switch.

The last brief asked Philip Morris to profit from ending the use — and ran straight into human nature on both sides. People want a molecule that removes the craving, not a willpower program. [ 01 / 05 ] Two Appetites

Two appetites you can’t lecture away.

Behavior change is a beautiful idea that loses to biology. People do not want to want less — they want a way to stop wanting that asks nothing of their discipline, which is why a weekly injection out-sold a century of willpower advice in about three years. Philip Morris’s own shareholders run on the identical instinct: they will defend the cash cow forever unless something bigger and easier walks through the door. You cannot moralize either appetite into submission. You can only build the thing that satisfies both at once.

Demand-side nature
People pick the pill, not the program.
A molecule that removes the craving will always out-sell a regimen that demands you resist it.
  • →Willpower is the one product nobody wants to buy
  • →No incentive scheme beats “take this and forget it”
  • →Ozempic is the proof: the demand is rabid
Supply-side nature
Shareholders won’t vote to shrink.
They will defend the cash cow until a larger, easier market appears — then they stampede toward it.
  • →A guilt-driven contraction never clears the board
  • →A bigger cash cow clears it overnight
  • →The pivot has to pay more than the poison
The off-switch is the only move that feeds both: a no-willpower magic shot for the user — and a category larger than nicotine ever was for the owner.
[ 02 / 05 ] The Inversion

Run the addiction map backwards.

Here is what no one else can say about Philip Morris: it is the planet’s foremost reverse-engineer of the human reward circuit. A century of work went into a single question — how to make the dopamine system want something, and keep wanting it. That is the exact circuit that nicotine, alcohol, opioids, ultra-processed food and gambling all hijack. One mechanism, many markets. The audacious pivot is not to find a cleaner thing to sell into that circuit. It is to take the map Philip Morris drew to exploit it and run it in reverse — and build the off-switch.

This is why the prize is Ozempic-scale, not nicotine-scale. The off-switch doesn’t address a substance; it addresses the shared machinery underneath all of them — so the category it opens is the union of every compulsion market at once. The early proof is already on the table: GLP-1 drugs, built for diabetes, turned out to quiet craving across food, alcohol and nicotine, and analysts now put that one accidental off-switch on a path toward roughly $100 billion a year by the early 2030s. Philip Morris is positioned to build the deliberate ones.

And it holds an asset no pharmaceutical company has: the demand-side intelligence. Decades of data on how craving is created, sustained, and — most valuable of all — exactly how and when people relapse. Pointed forward, the relapse engine that kept people hooked becomes a relapse-prediction engine that keeps them free.

[ 03 / 05 ] The Off-Switch

Four ways to switch addiction off.

Three already work in humans for some substance; the fourth is how Philip Morris would discover the rest. Each card names why it beats willpower, the market it opens, the precedent under it, the confidence we’d assign — and the trap an adversarial simulation has to catch before a cent is committed.

01

The Molecular Firewall

A vaccine — or a long-acting antibody — that binds the drug in the bloodstream so it never reaches the brain. The hit simply doesn’t arrive. A single long-acting shot, and the reward is gone.
DemandSet-and-forget. It removes the buzz without asking the person to resist it.
MarketRelapse prevention for people trying to quit — nicotine, opioids, cocaine, meth — then the firewall logic extends drug by drug.
PrecedentThe nicotine vaccine NicVAX failed Phase III — but on antibody consistency, not mechanism: the third of patients who mounted strong titers roughly doubled their quit rate. Fentanyl and opioid vaccines, and long-acting antibodies, have now reached first-in-human trials — the University of Houston’s fentanyl vaccine entered Phase I in 2026.
TrapIt blocks the reward, not the harm — and a determined user can try to override it with bigger doses, which is dangerous. Pair it with cessation, never with permission to keep using.
Hypothesis · mechanism shown, delivery unsolved
02

The Reward Dial

The “Ozempic for addiction.” A class of drugs that turns down the dopamine reward response itself — dampening craving across substances and compulsions at once, rather than blocking a single molecule.
DemandIt is the magic pill people are already lining up for by the millions. Proven appetite is the one thing this category is not short of.
MarketThe broadest of all — obesity, alcohol, nicotine and behavioral compulsions share the circuit it acts on. This is the bigger-cash-cow column, made literal.
PrecedentReal anti-craving signals across alcohol and nicotine (exenatide cut craving in trials; semaglutide shows it in real-world data); dedicated Phase 2 cessation trials read out around 2027. Researchers call the class the first true “anti-consumption” agents.
TrapIt is maintenance, not cure — a forever-pill for many. Commercially fine; just don’t sell it as an ending it isn’t.
Stated · signals, not yet proof
03

The Blocker Depot

A long-acting injection that occupies the reward receptors so the substance can’t activate them. Use it and nothing happens — the high has nowhere to land. Already real.
DemandA monthly shot built specifically to beat the adherence problem — you can’t “forget your willpower” when the protection lasts thirty days.
MarketProven in opioids and alcohol; the open build is the nicotine and poly-substance analogue Philip Morris’s delivery science is suited to.
PrecedentExtended-release naltrexone (Vivitrol) is FDA-approved — a monthly injection that blocks the euphoria of both opioids and alcohol by shutting the reward pathway, and profits precisely by ending the use.
TrapPeople stop taking the thing that removes their pleasure, and the blockade is “surmountable” with dangerous doses. Depot delivery and the right starting population are the answer.
Fact · approved for opioids & alcohol
04

The Discovery Engine

Fund an independent frontier-bio-AI lab whose entire mission is the off-switch — generative molecular and receptor design aimed at higher-titer firewalls, cleaner reward dials and longer-lasting blockers, trained against data only Philip Morris holds.
DemandIt is how every other card on this page gets better — and how the off-switch reaches substances no one has cracked yet.
MarketWhatever the circuit will give up. The moat is the data: a century of demand-side addiction intelligence, fused with frontier protein and chemistry models.
PrecedentThe three cards above prove the targets are real; what’s been missing is a discovery effort with patient capital, deep delivery science and addiction data under one roof — a gap a tobacco balance sheet can uniquely fill.
TrapFrontier bio is dual-use and the redemption is not automatic. It must run under the same biosecurity oversight and independent governance as any serious drug developer — never as a captive lab.
Hypothesis · frontier, governed
[ 04 / 05 ] What Happens When…

The reframe, run forward.

This is what the Foresight Engine does to a strategy: it follows the second- and third-order effects until the incentive structure either holds or breaks. Take the whole pivot as one move and watch where it lands.

Trigger
Philip Morris stops calling itself the world’s nicotine company and starts building the world’s anti-addiction company.
First order1°

It steps out of one shrinking market and into the union of every compulsion market — anchored by a GLP-1 category already on a path toward $100 billion a year. The supply-side appetite is fed without a guilt-driven contraction: this is a bigger cash cow, not a smaller halo.

Second order2°

The off-switch cannibalizes nicotine demand — but the loss is dwarfed by the category it opens, so capital migrates off combustibles on its own. The cash-cow problem doesn’t have to be won by moral argument; it dissolves the moment a larger, easier market is on the table.

Third order3°

Philip Morris ends up holding both the on-switch and the off-switch to human craving. That is the protagonist arc and the danger in one fact: the same reverse-engineering that built the addiction is what makes the cure possible — which is exactly why it has to be licensed, overseen and firewalled. The simulation’s job is to find the governance that lets the upside exist without minting a new monopoly over desire.

[ 05 / 05 ] The Honest Part

The off-switch is not a harm-switch.

Switching off the reward does not switch off the damage. A smoker whose buzz is gone but who keeps lighting up out of habit still inhales the tar. An off-switch sold as a licence to indulge — “use all you want, just take the shot” — re-creates the harm and the hypocrisy, and it is the first thing an adversarial model surfaces. So the rules around the molecules matter more than the molecules.

01

The firewall rule.

The same company cannot sell the on-switch and the off-switch to the same person. It is the direct analogue of “clean nicotine for the rich, cigarettes for the poor” from the essay — and it has to be a contractual, audited boundary, not a marketing promise.

02

The demand paradox.

People who don’t want to quit will not buy an off-switch — a product that asks someone to surrender a reward they still enjoy carries a built-in adoption ceiling. So sequence the markets where the appetite already exists: people who want out but can’t, and the compulsions no one defends — opioid use disorder, problem gambling, obesity — long before anything aimed at the smoker who is happy smoking.

03

Governance is the licence to operate.

A century-long addiction profiteer holding the master off-switch to human craving is a concentration-of-power question; frontier bio is a biosecurity one. Independent oversight isn’t a constraint on this pivot — it’s the only thing that makes it credible.

Fact

Approved and working in humans for some substance today. The antagonist depot. The question is the new build, not the biology.

Stated

Real signals, established direction, not yet proven for the target. The reward dial. The cessation read-outs land around 2027.

Hypothesis

Plausible, mechanism-backed, unproven at delivery or scale. The molecular firewall and the discovery engine. High upside, real risk — simulate before you build.

We will build the mirror, not the stone. We can price these futures, surface the traps three moves deep, and show which version survives contact with regulators, scientists and the public. We cannot make Philip Morris choose to switch the thing off. That part was always the point.
